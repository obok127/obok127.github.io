Working paper · completed Conversion Measurement

When denominator shifts make raw conversion rate tell the wrong story

V2U: Visit-to-User Normalization. In a 50-run direction-reversal stress test, raw visit CVR inferred the wrong direction every time, while V2U preserved the benchmark direction. The paper asks when visit-level aggregates can support a user-equivalent conversion interpretation—and explicitly identifies when they cannot.

Completed evidence: Eight simulation scenarios covering visit-intensity heterogeneity, segmentation, repeated converted visits, denominator-driven direction reversal, and telemetry loss.
Methodological contribution: An explicit unit-normalization framework for aggregate product analytics, with operational decision rules and clearly documented failure boundaries.
Validation status: Simulation study completed. External event-level validation on public or privacy-safe data is the next research stage.
Telemetry extension: Capture correction is treated as a separate problem from unit normalization; practical capture-rate estimation and sensitivity analysis remain follow-up work.
Artifact: 10-page technical report and 14-page reproducibility appendix.

Research in progress Probabilistic Metric Inference

Representing conversion uncertainty instead of hiding it in one number

PGF-CMI. An active draft exploring how incomplete, contaminated, or aggregate behavioral labels can be translated into probabilistic conversion estimates with explicit uncertainty.

Why it matters: A point estimate can look precise even when identity loss, selection, or telemetry quality makes the metric unstable.
Current stage: Problem formulation and experiment design; full estimator details remain private while the draft is developed.
Planned evidence: Synthetic benchmark, baseline comparison, robustness analysis, and documented failure conditions.

Issue intelligence system for data issues, ownership, timeline reconstruction, and source-grounded answers across Jira, email, Confluence-like documents, and collaboration history.

Decision timeline · source-grounded answers
ProblemData issues rarely live in one ticket. Decisions are spread across comments, emails, specs, old QA evidence, and follow-up discussions, so analysts spend time reconstructing context before they can act.
DesignNot a plain document chatbot. The system models data-issue history as a decision timeline with entities such as country, platform, page, variable, event, metric, owner, status, latest decision, and source evidence.

Tagging QA Automation Tool

AI-assisted QA workflow that compares UI/CTA evidence against tagging specifications and turns exhaustive page-by-page manual checks into alert-based review.

Manual inspection → alert-based review
ProblemFor each page, reviewers often need to open planning documents, tagging guides, UI references, and QA evidence manually. A single page can take at least half a day of one person’s time when every CTA and expected event must be checked line by line.
BuildUse UI screenshots or design references, tagging guide entries, CTA labels, expected variables/events, and image/text embeddings to match visible components with expected tracking rules.
OutputAlert report for likely missing tags, mismatched CTA naming, wrong variable mapping, duplicate coverage, or low-confidence UI/spec matches, so the reviewer investigates exceptions instead of re-checking every element.
ValueReframes tagging QA from exhaustive manual inspection into AI-assisted triage while keeping final approval with a human reviewer.

SEA to MST Rule Simulation

Implemented marketing-processing-rule simulation for migration QA, designed to prevent attribution drift before migrated campaign traffic is trusted by business reporting.

Pre-launch attribution drift prevention
ProblemDuring SEA to MST migration, marketing-channel processing rules must classify legacy tracking codes and CIDs correctly. If the migration rules are wrong, campaign performance measurement can become unusable or misleading.
BuildExtracted one year of historical tracking-code/CID data, attached SEA migration parameter scenarios, simulated the rule logic, and verified whether each pattern would be classified into the expected marketing channel.
ValidationChecked rule precedence, parameter coverage, expected outputs, mismatch cases, and high-risk tracking-code patterns before the migration was treated as report-ready.
ImpactPrevented business-facing marketing measurement issues before launch. The migration validation relied on this simulation as the primary check, while source code remains internal due to security constraints.
